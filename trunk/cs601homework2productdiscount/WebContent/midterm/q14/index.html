Answer To Question 14

What are the differences between GET and POST methods when an HTML 5 form to your server?

Many differences:

  1. The GET method encodes the form parameters into the URL of the call, the POST method encodes the form parameters into the HTTP header.
  2. For that reason, the parameters are visible in the URL on a GET, not on a POST.
  3. With POSTs, the browser will usually alert the user about re-posting the data.
  4. Since GETs are part of the URL, they can be bookmarked, including the parameters.
  5. The GET method has limitations on the URL length. IE has the lowest allowed length at 2k or so.
  6. The back-end has to use different functions to access POST and GET data.
